4 · Exchange-rate shocks read across: nominal eN · real eR · why · news vs level, real vs nominal

7 · Debt sustainability req. primary surplus / GDP = (r − g) × Debt/GDP

CaseWhat happens to Debt/GDPWhat's needed
r > gsnowballs: interest outpaces growthrun a primary surplus to stabilise
g > rfalls on its owncan run deficits forever; surplus required is negative
Treadmill: (r−g) = belt speed, Debt/GDP = backpack. e.g. r=4, g=2, D/Y=120% ⟹ need +2.4%. r carries a sovereign risk premium (self-fulfilling). Govt ≠ firm: no need to repay, just a non-explosive path.

8 · FX intervention CB buys foreign currency (defending a peg)

TypeCB balance sheetMoney baseMacro effect
Unsterilisedboth sides grow: FX reserves ↑ (assets), reserves credited (liab.)↑real effect ≈ monetary expansion (adds liquidity)
Sterilisedcomposition only: sell a domestic bond to soak the cash back upunchanged≈ none, just neutralises a portfolio shift
Asymmetry: defend a strong ccy forever (print your own); defend a weak one only till reserves run out ⟹ pegs break on the weak side (1992 EMS, Soros). Trap: "CB buying USD removes liquidity" → false (issues home ccy ⟹ base grows).

IdeaMechanism
Nominal anchorthe ONE nominal target the CB commits to (π~2%, FX peg, or money growth) to pin πe
Time inconsistencyfree to surprise-inflate ⟹ public anticipates ⟹ higher π, no output gain. Fix = rules + independence
