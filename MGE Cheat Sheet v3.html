1National accounts & identities

GDP: two ways GDP = PAQA+PBQB+… (final goods)  =  C + I + G + NX
National incomeGNI = GDP + NFP  (NFP = net factor payments: income from abroad − paid abroad)
The central identity (accounting, always true) Spriv=(GDP+NFP)−T−C   Sgov=T−G  ⟹ T cancels
S = I + CA   CA = NX + NFP   closed economy: S=I; world: CA=0

Rearrange for any trade question: CA = S − I. Surplus = country lends abroad (S>I, e.g. ageing Germany); deficit = borrows to invest (I>S, US). Not virtue/weakness.

Balance of payments & by sector Current Account = Capital & Financial Account
(Spriv−Ipriv) + (Sgov−Igov) = CA
FlowSignNote
You buy foreign assets / CB buys FX reserves+ outflowCA surplus = net capital outflow
Foreigners buy your assets− inflowCA deficit = net capital inflow
Traps. "Imports reduce GDP" → false (−M just strips out foreign goods miscounted in C). "Gov deficit ⟹ capital outflow" → false (depends on private S). "CA surplus + private inflow ⟹ reserves rising" → true (China 2013).

2Saving–investment & the real rate

The real rate r is the price that clears the market for funds. Saving = supply (↑ with r); investment = demand (↓ with r).

Fisher: real interest rater = i − π  (return in goods, not money; Japan 2005: i=0, π=−1.4 ⟹ r=+1.4)
Saving glut (Bernanke). ↑desired saving (China, oil, ageing) shifts world S right ⟹ world r falls AND imbalances widen. Long-run real rates are set by global S & I, not the central bank (myth).

3Growth: why countries are rich

GDP/capita decomposition (identity, each ratio cancels) GDP/Pop = GDP/Hrs × Hrs/Wkrs × Wkrs/WAP × WAP/Pop
= productivity × effort × employment × demographics

France ≈ US per hour (~$103 vs $105): the gap is hours + employment, not productivity. Growth ≈ productivity growth + population growth.

Exam. Pure qualitative, no Solow algebra. Japan "defies gravity": working-age pop fell ~12m yet labour force grew ~4m via later retirement + female participation ⟹ policy can offset ageing (with limits). Past T/F: "the two components that matter most are consumption and net exports" → false, it's investment.

4Money & inflation

The two aggregates & the multiplier Base = C + R  ·  Money supply = C + D  ·  MS = m·Base
m = (C+D)/(C+R) = (C/D+1)/(C/D+R/D) > 1

Panic ⟹ C/D & R/D jump ⟹ m collapses toward 1 ⟹ money supply shrinks with base fixed (Depression story).

Quantity equation ⟹ money-growth rule M·V = P·Y  (V = PY/M ≈ 1.67 US)
V stable ⟹ %ΔM ≈ inflation + real GDP growth
Money demand (real)M/P = L(GDP+, i−)  ↑ with transactions, ↓ with the nominal rate (opportunity cost of cash)
Long-run neutrality. Money is neutral ("add a zero"): CB forecasts real growth and chooses inflation. Inflation is ultimately monetary. Fixed supply ≠ stable value (Bitcoin volatile); a nominal anchor is a commitment, not a quantity cap.
Exam. "When China's CB buys US$ it removes liquidity" → false: it pays in yuan ⟹ base grows ⟹ adds liquidity (sterilise to undo). Initial price level is arbitrary; only the inflation path matters.

5Exchange-rate theory

Real exchange rate (foreign goods per domestic good) eR = eN·P / PF  eN = foreign ccy per domestic; rise = appreciation. eR<1 = your goods cheaper.
1 · PPP / Law of One Price (long run) eR=1 ⟹ eN = PF/P ⟹ %ΔeN = πF − πdom
Higher domestic inflation ⟹ currency depreciates (Italy vs Germany: lira −4.9%/yr).
2 · Balassa–Samuelson (productivity) Rich/high-productivity ⟹ higher price level (higher eR). Rising tradables productivity ⟹ real appreciation, via higher inflation if fixed or nominal appreciation if floating (CB chooses).
3 · UIP: currencies as assets (short run) r − rF = expected depreciation of domestic ccy
The key distinction. News of a rate rise ⟹ immediate appreciation; a high level of rates (≈ high inflation) ⟹ expected depreciation. A priced-in hike ⟹ no move. ~$10tn/day traded; capital flows, not trade, set the price.

Overshooting (permanent ↑M, sticky prices)

Exam. A fast-growing economy can still depreciate if its inflation is high enough (true). FX "theories" mostly fit ex-post: FX is far too volatile, rate-differential link weak.

6IS-LM-FX: the engine

Three blocks IS: GDP = C(GDP,r,T,W) + I(r) + G + NX(eR,GDP,GDPF)
LM: M/P = L(GDP+, i−)  ·  FX: r = rF
4-step routine (every time): ① shift the curve the shock hits → ② compare r vs r_F → inflow (r>r_F) or outflow → apprec/deprec → ③ adjust NX (apprec ⟹ NX↓ ⟹ IS back) → ④ repeat until r = r_F.
IS→ on: ↑G, ↓T, ↑confidence/wealth. LM→ on: ↑M, ↓P. r_F up: foreign rate rises.

8Taylor rule & supply side

The thermostat (Taylor 1993) i = (r* + π) + 0.5(π − π*) + 0.5·(output gap)
Taylor: r*=2, π*=2 ⟹ neutral 4%. φ's = how hard it leans.
#1 trap (Taylor principle). A 1pt inflation rise lifts i by 1.5pt, not 0.5 (π appears standalone AND in 0.5(π−π*)). The total response must exceed 1 or inflation spirals.
Overheating π=3, gap=0: i = (2+3)+0.5(1)+0 = 5.5%. Real r=2.5 > r*=2 (the brake).
Recession π=1, gap=−4: i = (2+1)−0.5−2 = 0.5%. Deeper ⟹ wants <0 (ZLB).

Reaction function, not identity. Tracks the Fed well 1982–2008. Long-run supply (LRAS) is vertical at potential.

9ZLB & unconventional policy

Deep crisis: low r*, deflation, big −gap ⟹ rule wants i<0, but cash floors i at 0. Stuck too tight ⟹ weaker demand ⟹ more deflation ⟹ self-reinforcing trap.

Deflation bites. r = i − π with π<0 ⟹ real rate soars (1931: 2.6−(−10.1)=+12.7%). QE floored nominal, not real, rate.
QE. CB buys bonds, credits reserves ⟹ both sides of balance sheet swell, base jumps (offsets a dead multiplier). Works via signalling + liquidity + lower long rates. QE ≠ inflation, ≠ financing the gov't (myths). Depression vs 2008: same trigger (multiplier collapse) but CB passive then vs expanded base now (deposit insurance held C/D flat).

11Fiscal policy & debt

Balances Primary balance = T − G − Transfers
Overall = Primary − Interest;  Interest = rnom·Debt
★ The one formula you compute: debt-stabilising surplus Req. primary surplus/GDP = (r − g)·Debt/GDP
r=4,g=2,D/Y=120% ⟹ +2.4%. If g>r ⟹ negative ⟹ run deficits forever, ratio still falls. r,g both nominal (or both real).
r carries a sovereign risk premium (can be self-fulfilling). Gov't ≠ business: default = redistribution, not destroyed wealth. "Must repay debt" / "high debt = high default risk" = myths.
