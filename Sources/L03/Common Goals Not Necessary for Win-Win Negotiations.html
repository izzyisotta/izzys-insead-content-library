Common Goals Not Necessary for Win-Win Negotiations

This article makes a subtle but important point: you do not need shared goals to reach a win-win outcome. Win-win is often misread as requiring that the parties want the same thing; in fact it is perfectly compatible with goals that directly oppose each other.

The mechanism is that a good agreement satisfies each side's interests better than its alternative (BATNA), not that it makes both sides agree on objectives. Two parties who want opposite things can still both be better off if the deal exploits their differences — in priorities, time horizons, risk appetite or valuation — to create trades that each prefers to no deal.

This reframes integrative negotiation as the search for mutually beneficial trades despite conflicting aims, reinforcing the 7 Elements focus on interests and alternatives rather than on finding common ground. Conflict of goals is the raw material of value creation, not an obstacle to it.

Key takeaways

  • You can reach win-win even when goals directly conflict
  • Success = beating each side's BATNA, not agreeing on objectives
  • Differences in priorities, timing, risk and valuation create gains from trade
  • Conflict of goals is raw material for value, not an obstacle
  • Focus on interests and alternatives, not on finding common ground
