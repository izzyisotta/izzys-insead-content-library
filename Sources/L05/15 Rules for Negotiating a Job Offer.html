15 Rules for Negotiating a Job Offer

Malhotra's HBR piece is the job-negotiation playbook the WABTY case rewards. Its through-line is that an offer negotiation is a relationship, not a one-shot fight: how you ask matters as much as what you ask for, because the people across the table will be your future colleagues.

Several rules recur in the course. Likeability matters — persistent but graceless demands get refused. Help them say yes by understanding the employer's constraints and showing why your request is justified. Negotiate the whole package, not just salary: signing bonus, start date, role, location and progression are often more flexible than base pay.

It also counsels reading intentions generously, not over-negotiating once you have a fair deal, and recognising that ultimatums are dangerous. The unifying lesson — find their constraints and the whole package beats the salary number — is exactly what the WABTY exercise is built to teach.

Key takeaways

  • A job offer is a relationship: how you ask matters as much as what you ask
  • Likeability and justified requests make it easier for them to say yes
  • Negotiate the whole package, not just base salary
  • Understand the employer's constraints to find flexible levers
  • Avoid ultimatums and don't over-negotiate a fair deal
