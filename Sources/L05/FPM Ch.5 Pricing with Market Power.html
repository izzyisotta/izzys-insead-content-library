L5 · MR=MC and the Lerner rule

FPM Ch.5 · Pricing with Market Power

This is the monopoly-pricing spine. A firm with market power faces a downward-sloping demand curve, so marginal revenue lies below price; profit is maximised where marginal revenue equals marginal cost. Selling one more unit requires cutting price on all earlier units, which is why the optimum sits left of the efficient competitive quantity and price exceeds marginal cost.

Rearranging MR=MC gives the Lerner index / Golden Pricing Rule: the optimal markup (P−MC)/P equals one over the absolute value of the elasticity. The less elastic the demand a firm faces, the higher the profit-maximising markup — pricing power is really a statement about elasticity.

The chapter then clears away common pricing fallacies: do not set price as a markup over average cost, do not chase market share or volume for its own sake, and never let sunk costs influence the price. The restricted output also creates a deadweight loss, the welfare cost of market power.

Key takeaways

  • Profit max for a price-setter: MR = MC, which puts price above marginal cost
  • Golden Pricing Rule: optimal markup (P−MC)/P = 1/|ε| — more inelastic, higher markup
  • Price off marginal (not average) cost; ignore sunk costs; don't chase share
  • Market power restricts output below the efficient level, creating deadweight loss
  • Pricing power is ultimately a statement about the elasticity of demand you face
