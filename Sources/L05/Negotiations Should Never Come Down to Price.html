S5–6 · re-expanding a one-issue deal

Negotiations Should Never Come Down to Price

This article explains why collapsing a deal to a single number manufactures win-lose. Once a negotiation is framed as price alone, every gain for one side is a loss for the other; the pie is fixed by construction and the only thing left is a tug of war.

The remedy is to re-expand the deal into multiple issues the parties value differently — delivery time, volume, payment terms, warranty, exclusivity, future business. Because each side weights these differently, trading across them creates value and gives both parties ways to come out ahead without simply conceding on price.

The deeper message is diagnostic: a negotiation that has 'come down to price' is usually a sign that the underlying interests were never surfaced. The skilled move is to step back, ask why price matters to each side, and rebuild a richer agenda — the practical complement to the interest-based 7 Elements approach.

Key takeaways

  • Reducing a deal to one number forces a zero-sum, win-lose fight
  • Re-expand to multiple issues the parties value differently
  • Trading across differently-weighted issues creates value
  • 'It's all about price' usually means interests were never surfaced
  • Ask why price matters to each side, then rebuild the agenda
