Stablecoins, Tokens and Currency Dominance

Rey examines how stablecoins and tokenisation could reshape the international monetary system. Stablecoins, almost all dollar-pegged and backed by liquid assets like US Treasuries, bridge conventional finance and crypto and are increasingly a cross-border payment rail, valuable where domestic finance is weak or sanctions/capital controls bind. The benefits (cheaper, faster remittances; a better store of value under poor governance) sit against serious risks: dollarisation, bank disintermediation, money laundering, fiscal-base erosion and the privatisation of seigniorage.

Because dollar stablecoins raise demand for Treasuries (Tether and USDC already hold more than Saudi Arabia), they could reinforce the dollar's "exorbitant privilege" as a digital pillar of US safe-asset supply. Tokenisation and CBDCs could unify ledgers and open cross-border asset access, but greater currency substitutability could equally fuel fragmentation, programmable capital controls and a more multipolar, fragile system, since unbacked private monies historically run.

Rey introduces an "integrity privilege": as quantum computing threatens cryptography, the currency network with the smallest cyber attack surface earns a financing premium. For MGE this updates reserve-currency, capital-flows and monetary-sovereignty debates for the digital era; international cooperation and data collection are urgent.

Key takeaways

  • Stablecoins are mostly dollar-pegged, Treasury-backed payment instruments bridging fiat and crypto; rising as cross-border/remittance rails
  • Risks: dollarisation, bank disintermediation, money laundering, fiscal-base erosion and privatisation of seigniorage to a few firms
  • Dollar stablecoins raise Treasury demand, potentially reinforcing the dollar's exorbitant privilege as a digital safe-asset pillar
  • Tokenisation/CBDCs could unify ledgers and widen access but also enable fragmentation, programmable capital controls and a multipolar system
  • New concept: an 'integrity privilege' for the currency network with the smallest cyber/quantum attack surface; private monies are historically run-prone, so cooperation is essential
