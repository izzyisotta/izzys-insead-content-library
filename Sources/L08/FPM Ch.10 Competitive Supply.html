FPM Ch.10 · Competitive Supply

Part III turns to the price taker. Because a competitive firm is small relative to the market, it faces a horizontal demand curve at the going price, so marginal revenue simply equals price; profit is maximised at P = MC. The firm's short-run supply curve is therefore its marginal-cost curve above average variable cost.

The key short-run decision is the shutdown test: keep operating as long as price covers average variable cost, because fixed costs are sunk and unavoidable in the short run; idle production only when price falls below average variable cost. This is why a firm can rationally run at an accounting loss for a while.

Summing every firm's marginal-cost supply horizontally gives the industry supply curve, which combined with market demand delivers the competitive equilibrium price that each individual firm then takes as given. This is the benchmark of productive and allocative efficiency the course keeps returning to.

Key takeaways

  • Price taker: MR = P, so profit max is P = MC
  • Firm short-run supply = marginal cost above average variable cost
  • Shutdown rule: keep operating while P ≥ AVC (fixed cost is sunk in the short run)
  • Industry supply = horizontal sum of firms' marginal-cost curves
  • Yields the productive- and allocative-efficiency benchmark
