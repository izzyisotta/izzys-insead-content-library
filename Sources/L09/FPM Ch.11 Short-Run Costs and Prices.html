L9 · entry, exit and the long run

FPM Ch.11 · Short-Run Costs & Prices

This chapter adds the time dimension to competitive supply through three price cutoffs. Idle if price falls below average variable cost (short-run shutdown); exit if price stays below average total cost (the long-run version, when even fixed costs can be shed); and enter if price exceeds average total cost, since that signals economic profit.

Free entry and exit drive the long-run dynamics: profit attracts entrants whose extra supply pushes price down, while losses drive exit that pushes price up. The process settles at the long-run equilibrium where price equals the minimum of average total cost and every firm earns zero economic profit (a normal return).

The Alusaf aluminium-smelter case is the worked application: whether to commit to a large, low-cost plant depends on the long-run price the new capacity will itself help determine, not on today's spot price. Long-run supply is therefore far more elastic than short-run supply.

Key takeaways

  • Three cutoffs: idle if P < AVC, exit if P < ATC, enter if P > ATC
  • Entry competes away profit; exit competes away losses
  • Long-run equilibrium: P = min ATC, zero economic profit (normal return)
  • Alusaf: base an entry decision on the long-run price, not the spot price
  • Long-run supply is much more elastic than short-run supply
