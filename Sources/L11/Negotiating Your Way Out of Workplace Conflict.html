Negotiating Your Way Out of Workplace Conflict

This is the core reading for the relationship-and-trust block and the Indonesia theme. It deals with negotiating against an aggrieved internal counterpart — a colleague or partner who feels wronged — where the emotional and relational stakes dominate the substantive ones.

Its central moves echo principled negotiation. Separate the person from the problem: address the relationship and the grievance before the substance, because a counterpart who feels disrespected will not engage on the merits. Look behind the position to the real interest, often visible in the other person's KPIs, incentives or sense of fairness, which explains what is actually driving the conflict.

Crucially, it argues you must repair before you bargain: acknowledge the grievance, restore enough trust to talk, and only then problem-solve. In ongoing internal relationships the relationship itself is part of the outcome, so winning the argument while losing the colleague is a bad deal.

Key takeaways

  • For internal conflict, the relationship is part of the outcome
  • Separate the person from the problem; address the grievance first
  • Find the real interest behind the position — often in KPIs and incentives
  • Repair trust before you try to bargain on substance
  • Winning the argument while losing the colleague is a bad deal
