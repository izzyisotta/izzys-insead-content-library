S13–14 · deadlines and the commitment stage

Don’t Let Time Pressure Dictate Your Options

This article is a warning for the commitment stage and for resisting sales pressure. Its core insight is that deadlines are often softer and more manufactured than they feel: many 'this offer expires today' pressures are negotiating tactics designed to stampede you into a decision before you have explored your options.

Time pressure narrows thinking, makes your BATNA feel weaker than it is, and pushes you toward the first acceptable deal rather than the best one. The article urges negotiators to test whether a deadline is real, ask who set it and why, and recognise that an artificial deadline can usually be questioned or extended without losing the deal.

The practical discipline is to protect your decision process: keep developing alternatives, do not let a clock substitute for analysis, and be willing to call a manufactured deadline's bluff. It connects directly to the course's treatment of commitment, credibility and how power is partly perceptual.

Key takeaways

  • Deadlines are often manufactured tactics, not hard constraints
  • Time pressure narrows thinking and makes your BATNA feel weaker
  • Test whether a deadline is real: who set it, and why?
  • Artificial deadlines can usually be questioned or extended
  • Protect your decision process; don't let a clock replace analysis
